How to Survive Midterm Week at ABAC

By ABAC Hub Team · Nov 12, 2025

Midterms can feel impossible when projects, quizzes, and group work all hit at the same time. This guide breaks everything into small, manageable steps so you can stay calm and still get the grades you want.

1. Map out all exam dates and deadlines

Start by listing every exam, quiz, and project deadline in one place. Use a weekly calendar and highlight the most important subjects. When everything is visible, you can plan realistic study sessions.

2. Use short, focused study blocks

Instead of trying to study for 5 hours non-stop, break it into 25–40 minute blocks with short breaks. This keeps your brain fresh and helps you remember more.

Tip: Focus on past papers, lecture slides, and your teacher’s review sessions. These usually give strong hints about what appears in the exam.

3. Prioritize high-impact topics

If a chapter is worth 30% of the exam, spend more time there. Don’t waste energy memorizing every tiny detail that might not even appear.

4. Take care of your body

Sleep, water, and simple food are more important than you think. All-nighters look cool but usually give you lower performance in the exam room.

5. Use your friends – smartly

Group study can help when you divide topics, teach each other, and share clear summaries. Just be careful that the “study” group doesn’t become a chatting group.
